The problem & my role
The problem
A platform that had outgrown itself.

Emirates NBD's digital banking product had grown organically over a decade — different teams, different tools, no shared language. The result: 13 button styles, mobile flows that were desktop UIs squeezed small, and a login experience that generated the most support calls of any touchpoint.

First-time users couldn't locate core tasks without help. There was no clear boundary between marketing pages and the secure zone. Accessibility failed across login, forms, and notification states.

My role
Lead designer, end-to-end.

I owned research synthesis, information architecture, interaction design, and design system build — across web and mobile. I ran heuristic evaluations, moderated usability sessions, and led alignment with 6 product teams simultaneously.

Embedded with engineering and a single PM, I was responsible for every decision from IA to final component spec.

Methodology

How we approached it

The work happened in two parallel tracks: understanding the current state, and building the foundation for what came next.

01

Site map of the current platform

Mapped every page, flow, and entry point across web and mobile. Identified duplication, orphaned pages, and flows that dead-ended.

Information architecture — as found
02

Discovery study with existing customers

12 moderated sessions with retail banking customers across different digital literacy levels. Prioritised based on transaction volume data.

03

Heuristic evaluation

Scored every major flow against Nielsen's 10 heuristics. Login and account opening scored lowest — these became phase 1 redesign targets.

04

Component & template audit

Catalogued every UI pattern in use. Consolidated 13 button variants to 5. Built the first version of the shared Figma component library.

Metrics

Metrics mapped to the critical drop-off points.

I worked with the product team to anchor the redesign to the three flows generating the highest support volume: account opening, login, and loan application. The 58% drop-off mid-way through account opening was the primary design target — users were abandoning after investing time filling fields, only then discovering they were ineligible. Closing that gap drove the rest of the work.

"Every decision in these flows had to work for two audiences at once: a first-time mobile banking user in their 20s, and a relationship manager walking a customer through it in-branch."

Before / After

Critical flow redesigns

We tackled the highest-friction flows first. Login, account opening, and loan application were responsible for the majority of support contacts.

Account opening

Before
1 2 3 4 5 6
1. Two navigation bars stacked — 8+ primary items compete for attention with the form task.
2. Left sidebar with account types pulls focus away — users abandon the form to explore options.
3. Eligibility requirements buried above the form — users only discover them after investing time filling fields.
4. 3-column layout collapses on mobile and creates unnatural reading order — eye travels across then down.
5. Date of birth split into 3 separate dropdowns — high interaction cost for a single data point.
6. Mobile validation format buried inside the label — users only see the error after they've already typed incorrectly.
After
1. Clear step indicator (Step 1 2 3) — users always know where they are.
2. Single focused task per screen reduces cognitive load vs. the original long form.
3. Warm hero + clear CTA replaces the impersonal dark background.

Loan application

Before
1. No sense of progress — single long form with no step indicator.
2. Numerical inputs only — user has no feel for what amounts or tenures are available.
After
1. Single focused task per screen — amount, term and monthly payment visible at a glance.
2. Live calculator replaces a multi-step form — users see their monthly cost before committing.
3. "Apply" CTA appears inline with the result — no separate confirmation screen needed.

Feature Savings

Before
3 2 1 4
1. No option for automatic saving on the backend — session expiry wipes all form progress.
2. Forms clear the current step when using the "Back" option — all input is lost.
3. No option to select a custom loan period — no real-time simulation available.
4. No counter for session expiry — users lose work without warning.
After
1. Goal-first framing — user picks saving target category before entering numbers.
2. Visual car selector with price ranges removes ambiguity around how much to save.
3. Live calculator with slider shows monthly contribution and credit eligibility instantly.
Outcome

What shipped, and what it changed.

The redesign rolled out across a 14-month incremental delivery. Beyond the usability numbers, it changed how the product organisation worked — from isolated teams rebuilding solved problems to a shared system that compounded over time.

  • Single Figma component library adopted across 6 product teams within the first quarter
  • WCAG AA compliance achieved across all primary flows
  • New feature design time reduced — teams stopped rebuilding solved problems
  • Design ops function established to maintain the shared library as a living artefact
